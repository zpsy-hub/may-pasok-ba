Our Hybrid Prediction System

We combine official government guidelines with advanced machine learning for best-in-class accuracy.

Step 1: Official Rule Check

We first check for automatic suspension triggers based on DepEd and PAGASA guidelines:

  • Tropical Cyclone Wind Signals (TCWS)
  • PAGASA Rainfall Warnings (Orange/Red)
  • Extreme Heat Index

Step 2: AI Pattern Analysis

If no rules are triggered, our AI analyzes complex patterns in historical data:

  • Multi-day rainfall accumulation
  • Ground saturation & flood risk
  • Historical LGU decision patterns
Why this two-step approach is better: Sustained monsoon rain might not trigger an official warning but can cause severe flooding. Our AI learns to recognize these nuanced, high-risk scenarios that rule-based systems often miss, ensuring better safety.

Understanding the Metrics

We use industry-standard indicators to measure prediction quality and reliability.

Precision (Trust)

When we predict a suspension, how often are we correct? High precision builds user trust.

Recall (Safety)

Of all actual suspensions, how many did we successfully predict? High recall prevents missing dangerous days.

F1 Score (Balance)

A single score that balances both Precision and Recall, indicating a well-rounded model.

Accuracy

Overall, what percentage of days did we predict correctly (both with and without suspensions)?

Our Priority: Safety & Trust. We optimize for high Recall (catching >90% of dangerous days) while maintaining strong Precision to ensure our predictions are both safe and trustworthy.
